Dossier Entry 01

Chapter I: The New Minimalism

The architecture of subtraction.

This collection explores the threshold where garment meets environment. By stripping away the superfluous, we reveal the raw structural integrity of Italian silks and heavy-gauge wools. Every seam is a calculated line in a broader spatial composition.

Dossier Entry 02

Chapter II:
Structural Form

"We do not design clothes; we define the space surrounding the body."

The second act of our winter odyssey focuses on the rigidity of form. Heavy felted fabrics are sculpted into geometric shapes that defy the traditional anatomy of tailoring.

Contrasting this structural severity are moments of extreme fluidity—liquid silks that drape and fold like water against a stone facade.
